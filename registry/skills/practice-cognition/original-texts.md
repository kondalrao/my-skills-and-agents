# Epistemology of Practice—Original 

Texts### "On Practice" (July 1937) 

**Knowledge originates from practice:** 
> "If you want to know the taste of a pear, you must change the pear and taste it yourself." "If you want to know the organization and properties of atoms, you must conduct experiments in physics and chemistry and change the state of atoms." "If you want to know the theory and method of revolution, you must participate in the revolution. All true knowledge originates from direct experience." 

**Two leaps in knowledge:** ** 

First leap—from sensory knowledge to rational knowledge: 
** > "The continuation of social practice causes people to repeatedly experience sensations and impressions in practice, thus giving rise to a sudden change (i.e., a leap) in the cognitive process in people's minds, producing concepts." "Concepts are no longer phenomena of things, nor individual aspects of things, nor their external connections, but rather grasp the essence of things, the whole of things, and the internal connections of things." 

The second leap—from rational knowledge to practice (more important): 
> "The active role of knowledge is manifested not only in the active leap from sensory knowledge to rational knowledge, but more importantly, in the leap from rational knowledge to revolutionary practice." 

**Practice is the sole criterion for testing truth:** 
> "Whether knowledge or theory is true is not determined by subjective feelings, but by the objective results of social practice. The criterion of truth can only be social practice." 

### "Where Do Correct Ideas Come From?" (May 1963) 

> "Where do correct ideas come from? Do they fall from the sky? No. Are they inherent in one's own mind? No. Correct ideas can only come from social practice, from the three practices of social production struggle, class struggle, and scientific experimentation." 
> "A correct understanding often requires repeated cycles from matter to spirit, from spirit to matter, that is, from practice to understanding, and from understanding to practice." 

### "Reform Our Study" (May 1941) 

**Oppose Dogmatism:** 
> "There is a couplet that describes this kind of person. It says: 'Reeds on the wall, top-heavy, light-footed, shallow-rooted; bamboo shoots in the mountains, sharp-mouthed, thick-skinned, hollow inside.'" "Those with book knowledge should develop in a practical way." 

**Oppose Empiricism:** 
> "Those with work experience should learn from theory, should read books carefully; only then can experience be made systematic and comprehensive, rising to the level of theory, and only then can they avoid mistaking partial experience for universal truth." **Seek Truth 

from Facts:** 
> "'Facts' refer to all things that objectively exist; 'truth' refers to the internal connections of objective things, that is, their regularity; and 'seeking' means that we study them. We must start from the actual situation at home and abroad, within and outside the province, within and outside the county, and within and outside the district, and derive from it the inherent, rather than fabricated, regularity, that is, find out the internal connections of the surrounding events as a guide for our actions."
